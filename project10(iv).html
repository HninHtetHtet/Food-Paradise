Chinese Fried Noddle (Plain Chow Mein)



INGREDIENTS

  • 500g noodles

  • 40g dried beancurd sticks

  • 60g fresh mushrooms

  • 60g winter bamboo shoots

  • 100g flowering cabbage

  • 10g scallions

  • 5g gingers

  • 6g salt

  • 3g monosodium glutamate

  • 4g soy sauce

  • 10g sesame oil

  • 35g peanut oil



PREPARATION

  1. Cut the mushrooms into slices, flowering cabbage into sections and bamboo shoots into pieces.

  2. Soak the dried beancurd sticks with warm water, wash them, then chop into pieces.

  3. Slice scallions and gingers into filiform pieces after removing their peels. Set them aside for later use.



COOKING METHOD

  1. Cook the nookles until they are well-done. Put them out and leave them to cool.

  2. Heat up the wok with peanut oil. Then add the noodles and stir-fry until they take on a golden color. Dish up and remove the oil.

  3. Get another wok. Spread a little peanut oil, and heat the wok to seventy to eighty percent hot.

  4. Drop the filiform scallions and gingers to stir into the wok.

  5. Add mushrooms, dried beancurd sticks, fresh bamboo shoots, and flowering cabbage and fry them thoroughly.

  6. Add the cooking wine, soy sauce, refined salt, and light soup and bring to a boil.

  7. Mix the fried nookles in and stir-fry together. Turn down the heat and braise with a cover for a while.

  8. Put in the monosodium glutamate and drop the sesame oil, and then the dish is done.
