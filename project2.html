Chinese Fried Rice (Yangzhou)



INGREDIENTS

  • 1/2 cup chopped carrot (very small)

  • 1/2 cup frozen peas, thawed

  • 3/4 cup finely chopped onion

  • 1 egg, lightly beaten (or more eggs if you like)

  • 2 cups bean sprouts

  • 2 tablespoons light soy sauce (add more if you like)

  • 3 drops soy sauce

  • 3 drops sesame oil

  • 4 cups cold cooked rice, grains separated (preferably medium grain)

  • 4 green onions, chopped

  • 8 ounces cooked lean boneless pork or 8 ounces chicken, chopped

  • about 10 tablespoons oil



COOKING METHOD

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon oil in wok; add chopped onions and stir-fry until onions turn a nice brown color, about 8-10 minutes; remove from wok.

  2. Allow wok to cool slightly.

  3. Mix egg with 3 drops of soy sauce and 3 drops of sesame oil; set aside.

  4. Add 1/2 tablespoon oil to wok,swirling to coat surfaces; add egg mixture; working quickly, swirl egg until egg puffs, flip egg and cook other side briefly; remove from wok, and chop into small pieces.

  5. Heat 1 tablespoon oil in wok; add selected meat to wok along with carrots onion; stir-fry for 2 minutes.

  6. Add rice, green onions, and bean sprouts, tossing to mix well; stir-fry for 3 minutes.

  7. Add 2 tablespoons of light soy sauce and chopped egg to rice mixture and fold in; stir-fry for 1 minutes more; serve.

  8. Set out additional soy sauce on the table, if desired.
