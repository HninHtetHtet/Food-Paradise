Mont Lone Yay Paw(Sweet Floating Rice Balls)

Ingredients

  • 400g glutinous rice flour
  • 100g rice flour
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 125 ml water
  • Drop of green food colouring(optional)
  • 200g palm sugar,divided into 1cm balls or chunks
  • 4 bird's eye chillies(optional)
  • Handful of fresh grated or dessicated coconut

Instructions

  1. Sift the flours and salt into a large bowl and mix thoroughly.Add the water and knead until it forms a pliable dough.
  2. If you want half the balls to be green,divide the dough into two,add the food colouring to one half,and knead again till the colour spreads evenly.
  3. Take a small piece of dough,roll it into a ball with about a 3cm diameter and the flatten it into a circle.
  4. Place a chunk of plam sugar in the middle of the circle and then roll the dough back into a ball so you cover the chunk of sugar up completely.
  5. Repeat for the rest of the dough till you have about 20-30balls.If you're felling naughty,stick a chilli into the odd ball.
  6. Fill a stockpot or large saucepan with water and bring to the boil. Once it's ready, drop the balls in one by one. They will sink to the bottom at first, but then float to the surface of the water when they're ready (hence the name).
  7. Remove the balls with a slotted spoon, and dish up on squares of banana leaf or saucers - you want 4-5 balls in each serving.
  8. Scatter the balls with the shredded coconut and serve whilst still warm.
