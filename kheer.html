How to Make Indian Desserts(kheer)

INGREDIENTS

  1. 1 cup of slightly overcooked rice
  2. 2 cups of whole milk
  3. 3 tablespoons sugar
  4. 1 tablespoon raisins
  5. 1 tablespoon chopped pistachios
  6. tablespoon chopped almonds
  7. 8 teaspoon powdered cardamom
  8. a pinch of saffron

Instructions

    Step 1

  1. Prepare the pan and your ingredients.
  2. Before you start cooking kheer, heat the pan and prepare the saffron and raisins so that they’re ready to use as you cook.
  3. It’s important to do this before you begin cooking so that your kheer doesn’t burn while you mix the ingredients.
  4. Before you start cooking kheer, heat the pan and prepare the saffron and raisins so that they’re ready to use as you cook.
  5. Put the raisins in a little bit of water to puff them up.
  6. Use any small or medium heavy bottomed pan that you have to cook the kheer.
  7. Heat the pan over a medium gas flame or electric stove setting.
  8. While the pan is heating up, grind the saffron and a pinch of sugar with a mortar and pestle. Set it to the side.

      9. Step 2
    1. Cook your kheer. Once you’ve preheated the pan and done the prep work, you’re ready to cook your kheer. Make sure to not leave the pan at this stage so that your kheer doesn’t burn.
    2. Add the cup of slightly overcooked rice and 2 cups of whole milk to your heated pan. Lightly stir this mixture.
    3. If you like a smoother texture, mash the rice slightly before adding it to the milk.
    4. Add your mixture of saffron and sugar to the milk and rice in the pan.

      Step 3
    1. Stir the mixture over the heat. You’ll need to make sure that you don’t leave the mixture in the pan at this point to keep it from burning.
    2. Keep stirring the pan until the mixture reaches a consistency that you wish.
    3. You should let the mixture thicken over the heat, but you can decide how thick it gets.
    4. Some people like their kheer thinner like a creamy soup while others like their kheer thick like oatmeal.

      Step 4
    1. Add the sugar and remaining ingredients. Once your kheer has reached the desired consistency, add the 3 tablespoons sugar and the raisins and mixed nuts. Fold these ingredients into the mixture.
    2. Add the sugar before the raisins and mixed nuts. You might want to taste the kheer at this point to see if it needs more sugar.
    3. Drain the raisins and add them to the mixture along with the 1 tablespoon chopped pistachios and 1 tablespoon chopped almonds.
    4. Remove the pan from the heat and add the 1/8 teaspoon powdered cardamom.

      Step 5
    1. Serve and enjoy! The time has come for you to enjoy your kheer! You can serve the dish either hot or cold, depending on your taste and the time of year.
    2. •Consider topping your kheer with a few chopped pistachios or almonds for the visual effect.
